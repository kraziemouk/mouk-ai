# Future Secure AI Resume Positioning Notes

## Role Target

Head of IT for a fast-growing global AI company moving from outsourced managed IT to an in-house function.

## Core Narrative

My background has been building scalable operational systems during periods of rapid change. While my title has been within Technology Operations, the actual work has spanned global support design, infrastructure readiness, vendor governance, identity and onboarding systems, operational reliability, and M&A integration. What interests me about this role is the opportunity to build an internal IT organization intentionally from the ground up while balancing scale, security, and employee experience.

## Best Positioning Sentence

I specialize in building scalable operational systems during periods of rapid growth and ambiguity. My experience spans global support operations, infrastructure readiness, vendor governance, onboarding systems, and operational transformation, and I am most effective in environments where structure needs to be built while the business continues moving fast.

## Reframe

Do not position as:

> Support operations leader.

Position as:

> Builder of scalable internal technology organizations.

## What They Are Really Hiring For

This is less a traditional Head of IT role and more a foundational operational executive role: stabilize, internalize, and scale technology operations during hypergrowth.

They likely need someone who can address:

- Operational chaos from growth.
- Inconsistent onboarding and offboarding.
- Fragmented tooling.
- Weak asset management.
- Poor documentation.
- Shadow IT.
- Overdependence on MSPs.
- Unclear ownership boundaries.
- Scaling pains across regions.
- Rising security and compliance pressure.

## Most Important Themes To Emphasize

- Hands-on operator and strategic builder.
- Build internal IT from outsourced/fragmented model.
- Global distributed support.
- Helpdesk, onboarding, offboarding, access, endpoint, and hardware lifecycle.
- Security-minded without being bureaucratic.
- Vendor and SaaS cost management.
- Documentation, policies, repeatability, and audit readiness.
- Calm under pressure with bias toward action.
- Partner to Platform Engineering, Security, Risk, Finance, Legal, and business leaders.
- Operational architecture, not just ticket execution.
- Workforce enablement and business continuity.

Hands-on IT and technology operations leader with experience building global support systems, scaling service delivery, managing vendors/MSPs, and converting fragmented support into structured, measurable, repeatable operating models. Strong fit for fast-growth companies transitioning from outsourced IT to internal ownership, with a leadership style grounded in clarity, trust, documentation, and operational resilience.

## MSP-To-Internal Transition Answer

### 1. Discovery

- Understand current MSP contracts.
- Identify undocumented dependencies.
- Review SLA performance.
- Understand employee and stakeholder pain points.

### 2. Stabilization

- Preserve operational continuity.
- Avoid changing everything immediately.
- Establish ownership clarity.
- Create visibility into workflows, assets, and access.

### 3. Internalization

- Prioritize high-value functions first.
- Build internal knowledge base.
- Gradually reduce MSP dependency.
- Hire intentionally around operational gaps.

### 4. Optimization

- Standardize tooling and processes.
- Improve automation.
- Mature security and compliance posture.
- Establish scalable support operations.

## Avoid Underselling

Avoid getting stuck in:

- Ticket queues.
- Support execution.
- Tool names without business context.

Elevate into:

- Operational architecture.
- Scaling systems.
- Organizational maturity.
- Service reliability.
- Workforce enablement.
- Business continuity.
