Hands-on technology operations leader who builds scalable internal technology organizations during periods of rapid growth and ambiguity.

Strong fit for fast-growth companies transitioning from outsourced IT to internal ownership, especially where the first Head of IT must stabilize the current state, stay hands-on, build the future team, and create scalable systems while the business keeps moving.

  • Establish helpdesk, onboarding/offboarding, endpoint, access, vendor, and documentation foundations.

MSP-To-Internal Transition Approach

  1. Discover: review MSP scope, SLAs, escalation paths, employee pain points, and undocumented dependencies.
  2. Stabilize: preserve continuity, clarify ownership, and create visibility into workflows, assets, access, and vendor performance.
  3. Internalize: prioritize high-value functions, build knowledge base and runbooks, and hire around operational gaps.
  4. Optimize: standardize tooling, improve automation, mature security posture, and establish scalable service metrics.

Leadership Philosophy

Build people. Build trust. Build systems that survive pressure.
  • Clarity over control.
  • Context over micromanagement.
  • People over process, but process matters when people scale.
  • Support is a signal layer, not just execution.
  • The system should survive your absence.
