## How I Would Approach The MSP-To-Internal Transition

### 1. Discover

- Review current MSP contracts, scope, SLAs, escalation paths, and performance gaps.
- Identify undocumented dependencies across identity, endpoint, onboarding, SaaS, hardware, and employee support.
- Listen to employees, managers, Security, Platform Engineering, Risk, Finance, and current vendors to understand pain points.

### 2. Stabilize

- Preserve continuity before changing the model.
- Establish clear ownership for tickets, access, devices, onboarding/offboarding, procurement, and escalations.
- Create visibility into volume, response time, resolution time, recurring issues, asset state, and vendor performance.

### 3. Internalize

- Prioritize high-value functions to bring in-house first.
- Build the internal knowledge base, runbooks, service catalog, escalation matrix, and documentation standards.
- Hire intentionally around the biggest operational gaps.
- Gradually reduce MSP dependency without creating service disruption.

### 4. Optimize

- Standardize tooling, workflows, access governance, endpoint management, and support processes.
- Improve automation where it reduces friction and risk.
- Mature audit readiness, security posture, vendor due diligence, and service delivery metrics.
